The typeface

I have just count’d ~25 conspicuously situated typefaces in my living quarters.

Let us say, such a fact, that it is the exemplar basis of my quandary in hermeneutics. The philosophers do not touch such a domain so much as expose its anxiety. Language, say, is a footprint. Yours, mine, ours, and so on. This is the inevitable end of humanity. It is much clearer now that automation has been elucidated so imaginatively with computer systems.

These typefaces say everything of my humanity. Class, madness, physiology, economy, potential, intellect, hopelessness, amorality, neural subsystems, modes of predictability. Qualities more or less opaque in each of us, in so many ways. Such is their existence, a volley —

What might I find outside?
